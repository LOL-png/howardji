About

Hi, I'm Howard! I'm a student at Stanford University studying robotics and artificial intelligence.

I'm passionate about developing intelligent robotic systems that can meaningfully interact with and assist humans. My research spans robot learning, computer vision, and medical imaging applications.

Currently, I'm working on collaborative human-robot manipulation at the CHARM Lab and generative robot learning policy at the Stanford Vision and Learning Lab.

In the past, I've developed a column-climbing robot for infrastructure inspection that earned me recognition as a 2024 Regeneron Science Talent Search Finalist. I've also published research on biological oscillator modeling in the PUMP Journal.

In my free time, I enjoy building hardware projects, participating in the Stanford Space Initiative, and exploring new applications of machine learning in robotics.
